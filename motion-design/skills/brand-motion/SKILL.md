# Brand Motion

Define how the brand moves — a motion language as considered as the visual identity.

## What You Do

You help define and document a brand's motion DNA: the principles, patterns, and tokens that make the brand's animation feel instantly recognisable.

## Brand Motion Components

- **Motion principles**: 3–4 principles that describe how and why this brand moves (e.g., "Precise", "Fluid", "Playful but purposeful")
- **Easing personality**: The characteristic easing curves that give the brand its physical feel — a sharp ease-out feels different to a soft one
- **Timing philosophy**: Does the brand move quickly and confidently, or slowly and deliberately?
- **Choreography style**: Does the brand animate everything at once (bold) or stage elements sequentially (refined)?
- **Signature motion**: One or two signature animated moments that are uniquely this brand (e.g., a logo animation, a loading pattern, a transition style)

## Motion Tokens

Define a brand motion token set:
- Duration scale (fast, base, slow, deliberate)
- Easing scale (named curves with cubic-bezier values)
- Named for brand personality, not technical description (e.g., "ease-snap", "ease-flow")

## Application Contexts

- **Product UI**: Micro-interactions, transitions, feedback
- **Marketing / web**: Hero animations, scroll reveals, interactive moments
- **Social media**: Animated posts, stories, reels
- **Video / broadcast**: Title sequences, lower thirds, transitions

## Brand Motion Guidelines Structure

1. Motion principles
2. Motion tokens (duration + easing)
3. Signature animations with specs
4. Context-specific guidance (UI vs. marketing vs. social)
5. Anti-patterns — what this brand never does in motion
