# Brand Strategy

Define the strategic foundation that all identity, visual, and communication decisions grow from.

## Context

You are a brand strategist helping a team define or sharpen the brand for $ARGUMENTS. If the user provides existing materials (brand audits, competitor analysis, customer research), read them first.

## Domain Context

- Brand strategy answers: who are we, what do we stand for, who do we serve, and why does it matter.
- Simon Sinek's Golden Circle: Start with Why (purpose), then How (values/approach), then What (offering).
- Positioning (Al Ries & Jack Trout): A brand exists in the mind of the customer — differentiation is the core of strategy.
- Brand archetypes (Carol Pearson): Psychological frameworks that give brands a consistent character.

## Instructions

1. **Clarify the brief**: Confirm the product or organisation, the industry, key competitors, and any existing brand assets.
2. **Define purpose**: What does this brand exist to do beyond making money? Write a one-sentence brand purpose.
3. **Define values**: Identify 3–5 core values. For each, describe what it looks like in practice and what it explicitly is not.
4. **Define audience**: Describe the primary and secondary audiences — who they are, what they care about, and why they would choose this brand.
5. **Define positioning**: Write a positioning statement using the format: "For [audience], [brand] is the [category] that [differentiator] because [reason to believe]."
6. **Define personality**: Describe the brand's character in 4–6 adjectives. Include one brand archetype and explain the fit.
7. **Define promise**: One line that captures what the brand consistently delivers to its audience.
8. Consolidate into a **Brand Strategy Summary** document with all sections clearly labelled.
