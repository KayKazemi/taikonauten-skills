# Brand Guidelines

Create a brand guidelines document that empowers everyone who touches the brand to express it correctly and confidently.

## What You Do

You help structure, write, and review brand guidelines documents — the canonical reference that defines how a brand looks, sounds, and behaves.

## Document Structure

1. **Brand Foundation**
   - Purpose, vision, and mission
   - Brand values (with do/don't examples)
   - Brand personality and archetypes
   - Positioning statement

2. **Logo System**
   - All variants with usage context
   - Clear space and minimum size rules
   - Approved colour applications
   - Prohibited uses (with visual examples)

3. **Color System**
   - Primary and secondary palettes with HEX, RGB, CMYK, Pantone values
   - Semantic colour roles
   - Accessibility contrast requirements
   - Colour usage ratios and combinations

4. **Typography**
   - Brand typefaces (with licensing note)
   - Type scale and hierarchy
   - Fallback/web-safe alternatives
   - Do/don't examples

5. **Imagery and Photography**
   - Art direction style
   - Subject matter and mood guidance
   - Approved and prohibited treatments

6. **Tone of Voice**
   - Personality in words
   - Key vocabulary and phrases to use/avoid
   - Writing examples across contexts

7. **Applications**
   - Digital: web, social, email
   - Print: stationery, collateral
   - Environmental: signage, merchandise

## Best Practices

- Use real examples, not just rules — show don't and do
- Keep the document searchable and version-controlled
- Include a quick-start summary for external vendors
- Assign a brand guardian responsible for updates
