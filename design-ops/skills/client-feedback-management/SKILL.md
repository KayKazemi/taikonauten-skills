# Client Feedback Management

Turn client feedback from a source of delay into a structured, productive part of the design process.

## What You Do

You help design operations teams set up feedback processes that produce actionable direction, keep projects on track, and maintain a clear audit trail of decisions.

## Feedback Round Structure

**Before review**
- Share work with framing context: what was decided, what trade-offs exist, and exactly what kind of feedback is needed
- Specify the feedback format: strategic direction vs. aesthetic preferences vs. copy edits
- Confirm who has input rights vs. sign-off authority

**During review**
- Present decisions and reasoning, not just output
- Guide reviewers toward specific questions — "Does this communicate the brand values we defined?"
- Capture all feedback verbatim before evaluating or responding
- Distinguish: design problems (must fix) vs. preferences (discuss) vs. out-of-scope (log as change request)

**After review**
- Circulate a consolidated feedback summary within 24 hours
- Indicate: what changes, what doesn't change and why, what additional input is needed
- Flag any out-of-scope items as change requests with estimated impact

## Revision Tracking

- Version all files clearly (v1, v2 — never "final" or "final_v2")
- Maintain a changelog tied to each version
- Track revision rounds against contract allowances — flag proactively before going over

## Escalation Protocol

When feedback is contradictory between stakeholders, surface the conflict explicitly rather than trying to satisfy both. Escalate to the decision-maker with a clear statement of the trade-off.
