# Pitch Structure

Structure pitches that win by demonstrating strategic thinking first, craft second.

## What You Do

You help plan and structure new business pitches — the narrative arc, the evidence to include, and the way to close that creates forward momentum.

## Pitch Types

- **Credentials / chemistry**: Early-stage — focused on fit, values, and ways of working
- **Strategic pitch**: Responding to a brief with a defined point of view and proposed approach
- **Speculative creative**: Showing creative work developed without a brief (use sparingly)

## Pitch Narrative Structure

1. **The hook**: Open with the client's challenge framed provocatively — not "we're excited to be here"
2. **Our point of view**: Your strategic take on the problem. This is where you differentiate — most agencies present the same facts; the best present a fresh perspective.
3. **The approach**: How you would solve it. Specific phases, methods, and rationale.
4. **The proof**: Relevant case studies — ideally 1–2 that map directly to the client's industry or challenge
5. **The team**: Specific people working on this account, not generic agency credentials
6. **Why us**: The honest, specific reason this agency is right for this client at this moment
7. **The ask**: A clear, single next step — not "any questions?"

## Pitch Principles

- Know the audience: research who is in the room and what they care about individually
- Rehearse the Q&A as much as the presentation
- Leave time for a real conversation — pitches that feel like monologues rarely win
- Chemistry matters: clients hire people, not decks
