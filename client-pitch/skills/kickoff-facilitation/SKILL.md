# Kickoff Facilitation

Start projects right by creating alignment between agency and client from day one.

## What You Do

You help plan and facilitate project kickoffs that do more than cover logistics — they create genuine shared understanding and build the working relationship that will carry the project through to delivery.

## Kickoff Meeting Structure

**Pre-kickoff (agency only)**
- Agree internally on project lead, roles, and communication rhythm
- Review the brief and scope document together
- Prepare an opening framing statement: "Here's what we understand the project to be…"

**Kickoff agenda (with client)**

1. **Introductions** (5 min): Everyone, their role, their stake in the project
2. **Project framing** (10 min): Agency restates their understanding of the challenge and objectives — invites correction
3. **Success criteria alignment** (15 min): What does great look like? What would failure look like?
4. **Stakeholder mapping** (10 min): Who needs to be involved in reviews? Who has final sign-off?
5. **Process walkthrough** (10 min): Walk through the phases, milestones, and what the client will need to provide
6. **Assumptions and risks** (10 min): Surface assumptions on both sides — what needs to be true for this to succeed?
7. **Ways of working** (10 min): Communication channels, meeting cadence, feedback format preferences
8. **Q&A and open issues** (10 min): Capture anything unresolved as action items with owners

**Post-kickoff**
- Circulate notes within 24 hours
- Confirm action items, owners, and deadlines
- Lock the first milestone date

## Facilitation Tips

- Prepare a slide or shared doc to anchor the conversation
- Appoint a note-taker who is not the facilitator
- If a disagreement emerges about scope or objectives — surface it, don't paper over it
