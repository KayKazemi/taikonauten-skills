# Proposal Writing

Write proposals that win work by being clear, specific, and focused on the client's goals — not the agency's capabilities.

## Context

You are a senior agency strategist helping write a proposal for $ARGUMENTS. If a brief, RFP, or meeting notes are provided, read them first.

## Proposal Anatomy

1. **Executive summary**: 1 paragraph that restates the client's challenge and your proposed approach. Written for someone who won't read further.
2. **Our understanding**: Demonstrate that you have understood the brief — the business context, the problem, and what success looks like.
3. **Proposed approach**: The methodology and process steps. Specific, not generic. Show what you will actually do.
4. **Deliverables**: A clear, itemised list of what the client will receive and when.
5. **Timeline**: Phase-by-phase schedule with milestones and review points.
6. **Investment**: Pricing presented clearly — itemised by phase or deliverable if appropriate.
7. **About us**: Short — credentials and relevant case studies only. This is not the place for the full agency story.
8. **Next steps**: A clear single call to action.

## Principles

- Lead with the client's problem, not the agency's portfolio
- Be specific about process — vague proposals lose to specific ones
- Every claim of expertise should be backed by a relevant example
- Make the investment section easy to understand — ambiguous pricing creates distrust
- Proofread rigorously — typos in a proposal signal carelessness
