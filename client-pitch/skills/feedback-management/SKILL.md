# Feedback Management

Turn client feedback from a source of chaos into a structured, productive part of the design process.

## What You Do

You help set up feedback processes that produce actionable, prioritised direction — and help manage situations where feedback is contradictory, out of scope, or unclear.

## Feedback Round Structure

**Before the review**
- Share the work with framing context: what decisions were made, what trade-offs exist, and what you need from reviewers
- Send materials in advance for asynchronous review where possible
- Specify the feedback format: are you looking for strategic direction, aesthetic reactions, or copy edits?

**During the review**
- Present decisions, not just outputs — explain the reasoning
- Guide reviewers toward specific questions: "Does this feel like it reflects the brand values we agreed on?"
- Distinguish between preferences ("I don't like blue") and problems ("This doesn't meet accessibility requirements")
- Capture all feedback verbatim before responding

**After the review**
- Consolidate feedback into a prioritised action list within 24 hours
- Flag any items that are out of scope as change requests
- Confirm: what will change, what won't change and why, and what additional input is needed

## Managing Difficult Feedback

- **Contradictory feedback from multiple stakeholders**: Surface the conflict explicitly — "We've received direction A from X and direction B from Y — which takes priority?"
- **Preference-based feedback**: Redirect to goals — "Does this solve the problem we set out to solve?"
- **Out-of-scope requests**: Acknowledge the idea, log it, and route it through the change request process
- **"I'll know it when I see it"**: Ask for reference examples and schedule a shorter, more frequent review cycle

## Revision Tracking

- Version all deliverables clearly (v1, v2, not "final", "final-v2", "final-FINAL")
- Keep a changelog linked to each revision
- Track revision rounds against the contract to flag when you're approaching the included limit
